WARPED PIXEL DUNGEON - PRIVACY POLICY
Last updated: July 14, 2026

Warped Pixel Dungeon ("the game") is a free, open-source roguelike developed
by GabriWar. This policy describes what data the game handles. The short
version: the game collects no personal data, has no ads, and no analytics.


DATA THE GAME STORES

- Save files and settings are stored locally on your device only. They never
  leave it, and uninstalling the game deletes them.


NETWORK CONNECTIONS THE GAME MAKES

- News and update checks: the game periodically fetches a public changelog
  feed and release information from GitHub. These are ordinary web requests;
  no account, identifier or personal data is sent. Both can be disabled in
  the settings.

- Multiplayer: when you choose to play online, the game exchanges gameplay
  data (positions, actions, the nickname you enter) with the server you
  connect to, only for the duration of the session. Nothing is used for
  tracking or profiling.


PURCHASES AND DONATIONS

- On builds distributed through Google Play, optional supporter donations are
  processed entirely by Google Play Billing. The game never sees your payment
  details; it only receives Google's confirmation that a purchase happened.
  See Google's privacy policy (https://policies.google.com/privacy) for how
  Google handles payment data.

- Builds downloaded from GitHub link to Ko-fi (https://ko-fi.com/gabriwar),
  an external website with its own privacy policy
  (https://more.ko-fi.com/privacy). The game itself is not involved in those
  transactions.


WHAT THE GAME DOES NOT DO

- No advertising, and no ad networks.
- No analytics, telemetry or crash reporting SDKs.
- No accounts, and no collection of names, emails, contacts, location or
  device identifiers.
- No selling or sharing of any data with third parties.


CHILDREN

The game does not collect personal data from anyone, including children.


CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

If the game's data handling ever changes, this page will be updated, and the
date at the top revised.
